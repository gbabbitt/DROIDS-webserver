3C MD

Webserver for Statistical Machine Learning Analysis of Comparative Protein Molecular Dynamics

What our webservers will accomplish

The 4C MD webserver is an ongoing collection of machine-learning assisted statistical tools for analyzing protein molecular dynamics (MD) in a site-wise manner. The raw trajectories must be generated by the user using Amber/AmberTools, NAMD, or OpenMM. We offer downloads for some simple to use GUI-based tools for generating your MD using these engines in concert with UCSF ChimeraX. Our servers utilize a wasm compiled version of cpptraj to locally resample your MD trajectories and bring the data to our AI, which in turn, enables 4 types of analyses. These include the site-wise (A) statistical comparison of MD from two functional protein states (i.e. DROIDS), (B) identification of functionally or evolutionarily conserved MD states, (C) indentification of MD that is coordinated across protein sites, and (D) indentification of complex bistable or multi-stable MD behavior associated with protein logic gating.

COMPARE protein dynamics

machine learning assisted analysis of comparative protein dynamics

_VIDEO-Introduction_ _VIDEO-Application to SARS-CoV-2_

DROIDS is a machine learning assisted statistical comparison of binary-state protein dynamics. It employs a Gaussian kernel classifier applied to bootstrap resampled MD runs from two comparative functional of evolutionary states (e.g. ligand bound vs unbound, wild-type vs mutated, or hot vs cold) It reports site-wise statistical divergences in MD, statistical significance of these diveregnces, as well as the maximum mean discrepancy in reproducing kernel Hilbert space for the learned features. Output includes both positional plots and color-mapped protein structures for these various metrics.

CONSERVED dynamics identification

machine learning analysis of functionally or evolutionarily conserved protein dynamics

The maxDemon program trains a multi-agent machine learning algorithm trained upon the protein dynamics in the two comparative binding states (generated by DROIDS above). By discerning the trained signal from random thermal noise across unlabeled MD deployment, it identifies sites and regions of functionally or evolutionary conserved dynamics. Conserved sites/regions are mapped to the reference protein stucture. Th program also enables further exploration and comparison of the impacts upon conserved dynamics caused by genetic variation of binding proteins themselves and/or the drug class variants they interact with.

COORDINATED dynamics identification

machine learning analysis of dynamics coordinated across protein sites

This analysis compares the consistency over time for the machine learning classified predictions generated by the comparative analysis of protein dynamics (i.e. DROIDS). The mutual information (via information theory) for the functional classification states compared across all pairwise sites is reported as a heatmap, identifying proximal and even distant sites whose functional classification states appear to fluctuated in a highly coordinated manner over the time of the MD simuatoions. Pairs of highly coordinated sites are also color mapped to the reference protein structure.

Because MD raw data is slow to generate even on high perfomance hardware, and is too large to move accross networks, we offer simple to use GUI preprocessors for both licensed Amber MD and open source OpenMM. These can be used to locally generate the MD simulations needed for our servers.

Both of these preprocessors utilize open source UCSF ChimeraX visualization. Other software dependencies include python kivy, perl tk, Amber/AmberTools, UCSF Chimera/ChimeraX, OpenMM, python sklearn, python matplotlib, and the R project for statistical computing.

Our downloadable software pipelines controls, but does not modify any other existing software applications. Please be aware of and follow licenses of these other softwares and cite them appropriately in publication.
